
\section*{Probability density function}
The Cauchy distribution has the probability density function (PDF)[1][3]

{\displaystyle f(x;x_{0},\gamma )={\frac {1}{\pi \gamma \left[1+\left({\frac {x-x_{0}}{\gamma }}\right)^{2}\right]}}={1 \over \pi \gamma }\left[{\gamma ^{2} \over (x-x_{0})^{2}+\gamma ^{2}}\right],}f(x;x_{0},\gamma )={\frac  {1}{\pi \gamma \left[1+\left({\frac  {x-x_{0}}{\gamma }}\right)^{2}\right]}}={1 \over \pi \gamma }\left[{\gamma ^{2} \over (x-x_{0})^{2}+\gamma ^{2}}\right],
where {\displaystyle x_{0}}x_{0} is the location parameter, specifying the location of the peak of the distribution, 
 {\displaystyle \gamma }\gamma  is the scale parameter which specifies the half-width at half-maximum (HWHM), alternatively {\displaystyle 2\gamma }2\gamma  is full width at half maximum (FWHM). {\displaystyle \gamma }\gamma  is also equal to half the interquartile range and is sometimes called the probable error.
